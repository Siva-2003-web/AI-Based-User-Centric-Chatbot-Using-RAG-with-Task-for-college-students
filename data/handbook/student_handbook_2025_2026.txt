# College Student Handbook

## 1. Academic Policies

### Course Registration and Add/Drop
- Students must register during assigned registration window. Late registration permitted for fees.
- Add/drop period: First 10 days of classes. After this, withdrawal with permission only.
- Withdrawal grades (W) appear on transcript but do not affect GPA.
- Maximum course load: 18 credits per semester. Minimum: 12 credits (full-time status).

### Grading System
- A: 90-100%, A-: 87-89%, B+: 84-86%, B: 80-83%, B-: 77-79%
- C+: 74-76%, C: 70-73%, C-: 67-69%, D: 60-66%, F: Below 60%
- GPA calculated on 4.0 scale. S/U (Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory) option for up to 2 electives per year.
- Grade appeals must be submitted within 7 days of grade posting.

### Academic Standing
- Good standing: GPA >= 2.0
- Academic warning: GPA 1.5-2.0 (meeting required)
- Academic probation: GPA < 1.5 (suspension possible)
- Dean's List: GPA >= 3.5 (15+ credits completed)

### Attendance Policy
- Students are expected to attend all classes. Extended absence (>3 classes) requires faculty notification.
- Excused absences: Medical, family emergency, religious observance, college-approved travel.
- Unexcused absences may result in grade reduction or course failure at instructor discretion.

## 2. Student Life and Conduct

### Code of Conduct
- All students must adhere to the College Code of Conduct.
- Prohibited behaviors: Cheating, plagiarism, harassment, violence, substance abuse, theft, property damage.
- Violations may result in warnings, probation, suspension, or expulsion.

### Classroom Conduct
- Disruptive behavior will result in removal from class.
- Electronic devices (phones, laptops) permitted only if used for class purposes.
- Respect for diversity and inclusion is mandatory.

### Housing Policies (On-Campus)
- Housing required for first-year students (exceptions: commuter, married status).
- Housing lottery for upper-class students held in March (announcement Dec 1).
- Move-in: First day of semester. Move-out: Within 48 hours of final exam.
- Guests limited to 3 consecutive nights in dorm rooms.

### Student Clubs and Organizations
- 100+ clubs available covering academics, sports, arts, service, and identity.
- Club meetings held in student center; contact Student Life for listings.
- Funding available through Student Government (approval process required).

- Math Lab: Peer tutoring for math courses. Hours TTh 10am-4pm, W 1pm-5pm.

### Health and Wellness
- Student Health Center: Primary care, vaccinations, mental health counseling. Hours M-F 8am-5pm.

## 4. Financial Information

### Tuition and Fees (2025-2026)
- Full-time students (12-18 credits): $15,000 per semester
- Room and board: $8,000 per semester (on-campus housing)
- Mandatory fees (technology, health center, activities): $800 per semester
- Parking permit (optional): $150 per year

### Payment Options
- Full payment due by date specified (typically 2 weeks before semester starts).
- Payment plans available: Monthly installments (interest-free, requires approval).
- Refund deadline: 10 days after semester start (prorated schedule thereafter).

### Financial Aid
- FAFSA required for federal/state aid consideration.

- Need-based grants available (application required).
- Work-study positions available on-campus (up to 10 hours/week).
- Student loans: Stafford and Parent PLUS loans information at financial aid office.
- Aid office: financialaid@college.edu, Student Center 210.

## 10. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What should I do if I need to withdraw from a course?**
A: Contact the Registrar's Office. Withdrawals processed within the posted deadline receive a "W" grade. After the deadline, withdrawal requires academic dean approval (can impact financial aid).

**Q: How do I request accommodations for a disability?**
A: Register with Accessibility Services (ODS) with medical documentation. Contact ods@college.edu or visit Student Center 200.

**Q: Can I change my major?**
A: Yes, contact your academic advisor or the registrar. A major change form must be approved by your advisor and new department chair.

**Q: What if I'm struggling academically?**
A: Contact academic support (tutoring, writing center) or meet with your advisor. Probation students must see their advisor every 2 weeks.

**Q: How do I appeal a grade?**
A: Submit a written appeal to the faculty member within 7 days of grade posting. If unresolved, escalate to department chair, then academic dean.
