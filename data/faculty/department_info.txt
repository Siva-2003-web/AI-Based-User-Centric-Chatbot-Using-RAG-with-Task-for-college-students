# Computer Science Department Information

**Department Overview:**
The Computer Science department offers undergraduate and graduate programs in computer science, with a focus on practical skills, research, and real-world applications. Faculty expertise spans AI, software engineering, databases, and systems.

**Chair:** Dr. Alice Johnson (alice.johnson@college.edu, Tech Building 300)

**Majors Offered:**
- B.S. Computer Science (4-year, 120 credits)
- B.A. Computer Science (4-year, 120 credits)
- M.S. Computer Science (2-year, thesis optional)
- Minor in Computer Science (18 credits)

**Core Courses (B.S.):**
- CS101: Introduction to Computer Science (3 cr)
- CS151: Discrete Mathematics (3 cr)
- CS201: Data Structures and Algorithms (4 cr)
- CS215: Computer Architecture (3 cr)
- CS241: Database Systems (3 cr)
- CS301: Artificial Intelligence (3 cr)
- CS321: Software Engineering (3 cr)
- CS361: Capstone Project (3 cr)

**Electives Available:**
- CS302: Machine Learning
- CS303: Natural Language Processing
- CS315: Cybersecurity
- CS331: Web Development
- CS341: Cloud Computing

**Research Opportunities:**
- Undergraduate research fellowships available (paid positions)
- Graduate research assistantships (tuition waiver + stipend)
- Faculty research areas: AI, ML, NLP, robotics, HCI, systems

**Career Outcomes:**
- Avg. starting salary: $85,000
- Top employers: Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Apple, JPMorgan
- 95% employment rate within 6 months of graduation
- Graduate school placement: 20-30% continue to PhD programs

# Mathematics Department Information

**Department Overview:**
The Mathematics department provides rigorous training in pure and applied mathematics, preparing students for careers in academia, industry, finance, and data science.

**Chair:** Prof. Michael Smith (michael.smith@college.edu, Science Building 200)

**Majors Offered:**
- B.S. Mathematics (4-year, 120 credits)
- B.A. Mathematics (4-year, 120 credits)
- B.S. Applied Mathematics (4-year, 120 credits)
- M.S. Mathematics (2-year, thesis required)
- Minor in Mathematics (18 credits)

**Core Courses (B.S.):**
- MATH101: Calculus I (4 cr)
- MATH102: Calculus II (4 cr)
- MATH103: Calculus III (4 cr)
- MATH201: Linear Algebra (3 cr)
- MATH215: Discrete Mathematics (3 cr)
- MATH301: Real Analysis (3 cr)
- MATH321: Abstract Algebra (3 cr)
- MATH451: Capstone Seminar (3 cr)

**Specialization Tracks:**
- Pure Mathematics: Analysis, Algebra, Topology
- Applied Mathematics: Differential Equations, Numerical Analysis, Optimization
- Data Science: Probability, Statistics, Computing

**Research Opportunities:**
- REU (Research Experiences for Undergraduates) grants available
- Summer research internships with faculty
- Graduate teaching assistantships

**Career Outcomes:**
- Avg. starting salary: $60,000 (industry), $50,000 (education)
- Top employers: Tech companies, finance, insurance, education
- Graduate school placement: 40-50% pursue advanced degrees

# Biology Department Information

**Department Overview:**
The Biology department emphasizes experimental biology and modern techniques, preparing students for careers in research, medicine, conservation, and biotech.

**Chair:** Prof. David Brown (david.brown@college.edu, Science Building 310)

**Majors Offered:**
- B.S. Biology (4-year, 120 credits)
- B.S. Environmental Biology (4-year, 120 credits)
- B.S. Neurobiology (4-year, 120 credits)
- M.S. Biology (2-year, thesis required)
- Minor in Biology (18 credits)

**Core Courses (B.S. Biology):**
- BIO101: General Biology (4 cr, with lab)
- BIO201: Cell Biology (4 cr, with lab)
- BIO211: Genetics (4 cr, with lab)
- BIO221: Ecology (4 cr, with lab)
- BIO301: Molecular Biology (4 cr, with lab)
- BIO311: Evolution (3 cr)
- BIO451: Senior Capstone (3 cr)

**Laboratory Facilities:**
- Molecular biology lab with PCR, sequencing, and bioinformatics workstations
- Microscopy lab with confocal and electron microscopes
- Greenhouse and outdoor field study areas
- Aquatics facility for aquatic organism studies

**Research Opportunities:**
- Undergraduate research grants ($1,000-$2,000 per project)
- Summer internships in research labs
- Graduate teaching and research assistantships

**Career Outcomes:**
- Avg. starting salary: $45,000 (entry), $65,000+ (advanced degree)
- Top employers: Pharmaceutical companies, biotech startups, research institutes, healthcare
- Medical/Dental school acceptance rate: 70%+
